Introduction

You are invited to take part in a research study. Before you decide, you need to understand why the research is being done and what it would involve for you. Please take the time to read the following information carefully.

Please contact the researcher or supervisor if there is anything that is not clear or if you would like more information. Take time to decide whether or not you wish to take part.

Who is organising and funding the study?

This study is being conducted by Esha Patel, a student at the University of Warwick, as part of an undergraduate dissertation project in cybersecurity. The study is supervised by Dr Hany Atlam.

The research is conducted solely for academic purposes and is not funded by any external organisation or commercial body.

What is the study about?

The purpose of this study is to investigate the usability and security of a graphical authentication system.

Traditional authentication systems rely on text-based passwords, which can be difficult for users to remember and may be vulnerable to security threats such as guessing or reuse across multiple systems.

This study explores an alternative authentication approach using images and visual patterns combined with additional verification steps. The aim is to understand whether this type of authentication can provide a secure and user-friendly login experience.

What would taking part involve?

If you choose to participate, you will interact with a prototype authentication system.

  1. Read this Participant Information Leaflet and provide consent.
  2. Register an account by creating a username and selecting a graphical password.
  3. Attempt to log in using the graphical authentication system.
  4. Complete a short recall sequence for authentication.
  5. Entering a verification code generated by the system.

The activity will take approximately 5–10 minutes. Participation takes place entirely online.

No audio recordings, video recordings, or interviews will take place.

Do I have to take part?

No. Participation is completely voluntary. You may stop taking part at any time by simply closing the browser window.

What are the possible benefits of taking part?

There are no direct personal benefits. However, participation contributes to research investigating new authentication methods designed to improve both security and usability.

What are the possible risks?

The study involves minimal risk. The task involves interacting with a prototype web-based authentication system and will take approximately 5–10 minutes.

No sensitive personal information will be collected.

Expenses and payments

Participants will not receive payment or reimbursement for taking part.

Will my taking part be kept confidential?

Yes. All data collected during this study will be treated confidentially. Participants will create a username of their choice which will not be linked to their real identity.

Data will be stored securely and only accessed by the researcher and their supervisor. All results will be reported in an anonymous and aggregated form.

What happens if I withdraw?

You may stop participating at any time by closing the browser window. Once data has been anonymised and included in the research analysis, it may not be possible to remove individual contributions.

What will happen to the results?

The results will form part of an undergraduate dissertation at the University of Warwick and may be presented in academic publications or presentations.

Who has reviewed the study?

This study has been reviewed by the University of Warwick Humanities and Social Sciences Research Ethics Committee (HSSREC).
